# Abstract
Autonomous mine transportation is a representative large-scale cyber-physical system operating under highly dynamic environments. Efficient scheduling requires continuous perception of traffic conditions and adaptive decision-making.
This project investigates an AI-driven scheduling framework integrating reinforcement learning, spatio-temporal traffic intelligence, and intelligent decision-making for autonomous mine transportation. The research aims to improve scheduling efficiency and transportation reliability under complex dynamic environments.

---
# Research Motivation
Open-pit mine transportation involves heterogeneous vehicles, dynamic traffic conditions, and continuously changing operational constraints.
Traditional rule-based scheduling methods often struggle to adapt to these environments.
This research explores how artificial intelligence can support adaptive scheduling through reinforcement learning and traffic intelligence.

---
# Research Questions
This project focuses on the following research questions.
**RQ1**
How can reinforcement learning improve scheduling efficiency in dynamic mine transportation systems?
**RQ2**
How can traffic conditions be recognized continuously from large-scale trajectory data?
**RQ3**
How can intelligent scheduling adapt to continuously changing transportation environments?
